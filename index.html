Airbnb is an online marketplace and hospitality service, enabling people to lease or rent short-term lodging including vacation rentals, apartment rentals, homestays, hostel beds, or hotel rooms. Airbnb connects people to unique travel experiences, at any price point, in more than 33,000 cities and 192 countries.

Industry: Travel, Hospitality

Use cases: Funnel analysis, Categorized User Querying, Scroll Tracking, AWS S3 Configuration, Targeting Host Preferences

Funnel Analysis

Airbnb’s users love the platform not only due to the ease of booking, but due to the high number and wide variety of spaces available to rent. By utilizing Heaps’ funnels, they can optimize the process the booking process for users or potential hosts go through as they list their space.

By using funnels, Airbnb can figure out where and when their losing users. They can dig deeper and compare conversion rates between user segments. When initially searching for a place to rent, are the fields “anywhere”, “anytime”, “# of guests” an effective process to book? How many users have spent a large amount of activity in “window shopping” places to rent versus booking? These questions can lead to process optimization using Heap’s funnels.

Categorizing User Querying

Visitors have the option of researching rentals using Airbnb as a registered user and they can start tracking their actions or as an anonymous user which might indicate passive browsing. It is difficult to measure conversion rates on anonymous users. Heap generates a random unique user ID and stores it in a cookie, or on iOS, in NSUserDefaults. These users will appear in the list view and are known as anonymous users. With Heap’s identify API, Airbnb can start tracking their users to analyze which featured contents have proved to be very popular and which features aren’t receiving enough user action. Heap SQL can measure metrics such as the average time on site to analyze how long user retention has been verified within Airbnb.

Scroll Tracking

Airbnb features content within a few categories on their website. These categories include recommended listings, just booked, viewed by your friends, homes, and featured destinations. They can carefully architect the structure of scroll tracking to conduct content analysis by implementing Heap’s client-side interaction code. By capturing more granular scroll data, Airbnb can determine where their users start to drop off by browsing their featured content. They can start by firing events based on specific impressions of their featured destinations towards the footer of the homepage.

AWS S3 Configuration

Airbnb is using Amazon Simple Storage Service (Amazon S3) to house backups and static files, including 10 terabytes of user pictures. Once enabling data exports within Heap environments, Airbnb can configure AWS S3 with Heap to add an additional layer of analysis since Heap offers the ability to export information for Airbnb’s data pipeline.

Targeting Host Preferences

Combining each of Heap’s features leads to a process that concludes with definitive analysis results. For example, Airbnb can begin to ask the question on how hosts determine their preferences and similar patterns that appear. Determining check-in and check-out ranges can be utilized to find a correlation between popular days of the week and peaks in price points. Using Heap to determine a roadmap on defining the events, running queries, and creating reports will lead to an optimized process for comparing user events.
